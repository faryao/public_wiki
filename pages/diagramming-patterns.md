title: Diagramming patterns
summary: Reusable visual structures for systems, decisions, and stories.

## Start with the question

A diagram is useful when it answers a specific question. Write that question at the top before adding boxes. If an element does not help answer it, move the element aside.

## Three dependable patterns

### Flow

Arrange steps from left to right. Use diamonds only for real decisions and label outgoing arrows with their conditions.

### Map

Put the central concept in the middle and group related ideas spatially. Distance should communicate meaning, not happen by accident.

### Layers

Use horizontal bands for responsibility, system boundaries, or levels of abstraction. Keep the direction of movement consistent.

## Make it readable

Prefer alignment and whitespace over decoration. Use one accent color for emphasis and keep labels short enough to scan.
